**Why REJECT and not NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF:**

The `proof-gate-v1` schema does not carry a `NEEDS_LIVE_CONTEXT` verdict — that option only exists in `sentinel-triage-v1`. [1](#0-0)  The proof gate's only valid verdicts are `REJECT`, `NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF`, and `HIGH_CONFIDENCE_CANDIDATE`. [2](#0-1) 

The proof gate is designed to receive input only from `needs_local_proof/` and `deepwiki_candidates/` — not from `needs_live_context/`. [3](#0-2)  This candidate was correctly routed to `needs_live_context/` by triage and should not have entered the proof gate queue yet. [4](#0-3) 

The three blocking hard gates — `exact_code_path_or_selector_exists`, `attacker_controls_trigger`, and `concrete_fund_or_reward_gain` — all require a confirmed public selector, which is impossible without bytecode recovery. The hard rule from triage applies here too: prefer REJECT over speculation. [5](#0-4)
